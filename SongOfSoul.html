The Return of Magic

A Song of Soul and Spirit

A story of rediscovery, resonance, and divine communion.

I’d like to take you into a story, not just mine, but ours. A story about singing, but not the kind you rehearse in front of mirrors or perform under stage lights. This is about a deeper kind of song. The kind that remembers you when you’ve forgotten yourself. The kind that reaches beyond melody and harmony and stirs the soul awake.

It began quietly, in the most ordinary of places: rehearsal. I was in a Christian band, learning songs. The notes were there, the lyrics were fine, but something in me had gone silent. I couldn’t care. Not out of rebellion or laziness, but because something essential was missing. I wasn’t justasking how to sing better. I was asking:

Where did the magic go?

A Whisper from Within

That longing cracked open a door. In a session of deep listening, a sacred space, I asked myself not what to do, but what I was missing. What returned wasn’t instruction. It was memory.

The magic wasn’t gone. It had just been waiting.

Magic, as I remembered it, wasn’t loud. It wasn’t about impressing anyone. It lived in those moments where I didn’t feel pressure to get it right, where emotion flowed not from control, but from surrender.

It was the soul responding to the gentle movement of Spirit, not an idea, but a living, breathing force within. And my body? It didn’t need to understand it. It knew it through nerve endings and presence, through a felt sense.

That felt sense lit a path to intuition. And that intuition revealed something holy: A knowing of experience, flowing from Source, from All that is.

In that alignment of spirit, soul, body, and knowing, life unfolded. Not as theory. As my life. And it was rich. Vibrant. Beautiful.

The Song That Sings You

With this magic awakened, singing changed. It became:

  • Light as a feather, but full of resonance
  • Effortless, yet deeply supported
  • Not just sound, but space: the presence of space, the space of presence
  • Timeless

The magic wasn’t a technique. It was a return. Not just to music, but to being.

The Garden Where Magic Grows

But magic doesn’t bloom in isolation. It requires something precious: Safety. Connection. Belonging.

Just before the magic returns, I feel safe among others. Not just socially, but spiritually:

  • Trust lives in the room
  • Every nonverbal cue matches every word
  • There’s no fear. No chaos. Only stillness and unity

This is what we call the Field of Unity. It’s a place of magnetic awareness, alive in us and through us.

And in this field, the song isn’t sung by me.
It sings through me.

My voice, my body, my being, all are animated. I become part of something larger: a light emanating force, birthing not just harmony, but authentic community. And this community chooses something radical in today’s world: To surrender, grow, and move as one.

Whole and Healed

When that happens, life feels cinematic. Like I’ve stepped into a film where every frame is sacred.

  • I feel whole
  • I no longer feel lack
  • I can focus, not by force, but by presence
  • I see others clearly, with empathy

And that empathy? It strengthens the field. The loop continues.

The Voice of God

Then, something even more beautiful happens. In this space, this unity, this trust, I don’t just sing. I dissolve. I become the voice of God.

This isn’t metaphor. It’s communion. And that voice? It doesn’t just echo off walls. It gives life.

Others feel it. And they are healed. Whole and complete.

A New Normal

This is my prayer.

That this magic, not as an escape but as a return, becomes normal. Sustainable. Without doubt. Without resistance.

Not a fleeting gift, but a daily rhythm.
A consistent default. A new status quo.

A world where the magic of singing is always present. Where every soul can respond to Spirit. Where every body can know it. Where every song becomes a doorway back to ourselves and to each other.

Let’s remember the magic.

Let’s keep singing.

Thank you.
